- 2026-07-19 — **Phase 5 done.** `src/lib/charts/polar.ts` (projection) and `all-sky.ts` (model), plus `AllSkyChart.svelte` under `src/components/`, filling the "All-sky view" slot. 48 new tests, 288 total.   - **The track is cut at the rim, not clamped to it** — the opposite of `altitudeToY`'s deliberate clamp on the altitude chart. There, a set object running flat along the baseline reads correctly; on a dial, clamping would smear the whole below-horizon pass around the rim as a fake arc. `aboveHorizonArcs` splits the track into passes instead, and interpolates the sample either side of each crossing down to altitude 0 so an arc does not visibly start short of the edge.

- 2026-07-19 — **Phase 6 done.** `src/lib/astro/events.ts` (object events + the combined `nightEvents` model) and `moon.ts`, plus `EventTimesPanel.svelte` filling the "Times and directions" slot. 41 new tests, 329 total.
  - **The two horizons are found two different ways, on purpose.** Altitude 0° goes through astronomy-engine's `SearchRiseSet`; the observer's measured horizon is scanned for sign changes of `altitude(t) − horizonAltitude(azimuth(t))` and bisected to the second. No library search can solve a threshold that varies with azimuth, and `SearchRiseSet` knows things a scan does not — see the Moon below. A test pins the two against each other on a flat horizon: they agree to well under a minute, which is what licenses trusting the scan on a measured profile.
  - **Moonrise is not "the Moon's centre reaches 0°".** It is the upper limb, and with the semidiameter that is ~2–6 minutes earlier; a centre-altitude search was written first and looked fine until a test compared the two. That is why the Moon does not go through the generic scan, and why the Moon *as a target* (Phase 8) will read a couple of minutes off its own moonrise row — flag if that matters.
  - **Transit is solved with `SearchHourAngle`, not read off the trajectory.** The peak of a smooth curve is exactly where sampling is least informative: a 5-minute step rounds the transit time to 5 minutes and understates the altitude. Null is a real answer here — the Moon's transits are ~24h50m apart, so some noon→noon windows contain none.
  - Rise/set take the **first crossing each way** from window start, matching `computeSunEvents`. An object with a sidereal day shorter than the window can rise twice; the first-crossing rule picks out the evening's rise and the morning's set for anything observed around midnight. The observer-horizon rows instead take the **first** rise and the **last** set across all spans, so a jagged horizon that briefly re-blocks an object still reports the useful bracket. All spans are exposed on the model rather than collapsed.
  - `astro/` now imports from `horizon/` — the first such edge. A horizon is a property of the observer like the location, and the alternatives (a clearance callback, a second copy of the interpolation) were worse. `MOON` moved from `catalog/solar-system.ts` to `astro/moon.ts` for the same reason `SUN` lives in `astro/sun.ts`; the catalog re-exports it, so it is still searchable.
  - **What the panel says when an event does not happen is most of the work.** "circumpolar — always up", "never rises", "stays blocked" and "polar day" are all answers, and a dash for each would lose the difference. The circumpolar phrase is worded to sit on both the rise and the set row. Observer-horizon rows are hidden entirely when the horizon is flat, where they would only restate rise and set.
  - Times are forced to a **24-hour clock** (`hourCycle: 'h23'`) regardless of locale: they sit directly under a chart axis labelled 12/15/18/21, and 08:06 PM beside a tick labelled 20 makes the reader convert. Times past midnight carry a `+1`, since the window runs noon→noon.
  - The panel's three columns are `minmax(13rem, 1fr)` — the theme's root font is ~18.7px, so 14rem already wrapped the Moon onto a second row at the results-panel width. A visual test pins that all three groups share a row and that no value is clipped.
